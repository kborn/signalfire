# Project Context

## Purpose

This project has two goals:

1. **Professional development**
2. **Real product creation**

The system should both improve the developer's skills and produce a usable public platform.

---

# Platform Description

SignalFire is a **civil action platform** that helps people:

- learn about civic issues
- discover meaningful actions
- participate in real-world civic engagement

The platform connects knowledge with action.

Users should be able to move from:

Learn → Decide → Act

However users may also enter the system through actions, events, or topics.

---

# Core Platform Content

## Topics

Topics represent broad civic issue areas.

Examples:

- consumer activism
- democracy
- climate
- labor
- education

Topics organize:

- articles
- actions
- events

---

## Articles

Articles provide long-form explanations of issues.

Examples:

- blog posts
- issue explainers
- educational content

Articles may link to related actions or events.

---

## Actions

Actions represent concrete steps users can take.

Examples:

- boycott a company
- donate
- contact representatives
- volunteer

Actions are curated by administrators in Release 1.

---

## Events

Events represent scheduled real-world participation opportunities.

Examples:

- protests
- rallies
- volunteer opportunities
- town halls

Events can be discovered directly or through topics and articles.

---

# Community Contributions

Visitors may submit:

- events
- articles

Submissions are moderated before publication.

---

# Product Constraints

SignalFire is not:

- a social network
- a comment-driven discussion forum

The platform focuses on **civic participation and action**.
